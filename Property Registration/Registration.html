About Property Registration

In India, property registration is mandatory under Section 17 of the Registration Act 1908 for the purchase or sale of any immovable property. If the government records do not have the buyer's name on them, the property is deemed to not be owned by the said buyer and, therefore, cannot be challenged in court.


Laws on registration of property

Under the Indian property laws, two main laws are enforced in terms of property ownership or transfer and must be followed diligently by every Indian citizen.

  • Registration Act, 1908: Under the Registration Act of 1908, an entity purchasing or selling any immovable property of more than ₹ 100 is obligated to register the property with the Government of India. Not doing so is a punishable offence.
  • Indian Stamp Act, 1889: This law makes the payment of stamp duty mandatory on the purchase of a new property.

    • New rules for property registration in India

    There were some adjustments made by the government under the property laws of India in 2020. The laws have been set in place to ease the process of property transfers between parties. Some of the changes in the laws are mentioned below.

  • Since all the records will be computerised, the party making the sale or purchase will receive all the necessary documents on the same day.
  • If your property is not registered with the government, you cannot ask for any Income Tax Return compensation.
  • The property will have no validation in court if it is not registered. For example, if there is a land dispute and your property is not registered, you cannot take it up in court.

    • Things to check before Property Registration

    Here is a list of things you need to keep in check if you are going to register your property with the government.

  • You are required to check all the documents regarding the ownership of the property before you put your name down as a new buyer. A title search is a must.
  • Make sure all the utility bills are cleared before you buy the new property.
  • Calculate the stamp duty according to the regulations of your state, since stamp duty differs state-wise.
  • You need to get a deed made that includes all the terms and conditions of the buyer and seller.
  • Getting an encumbrance certificate is vital to making sure there are no legal burdens on the property.

    • Documents required for Property Registration

    Documents required for property registration are mentioned below.

  • Passport-size photographs of the seller and the purchaser
  • Aadhaar Card and PAN card as identification proof
  • Latest copy of the property registers
  • Power of Attorney
  • Municipality bill copy
  • No Objection Certificate
  • Verified copy of the Sales Deed
  • Certificate of construction completion
  • Stamp Duty and registration fee payment receipt

    • Property Registration Process

    The property registration process is mentioned below.

  • Step 1:The first step is to calculate the property value based on the circle rate of the area
  • Step 2:After estimation, you are to purchase a non-judicial stamp paper to sign the deed on the paper.
  • Step 3:Visit the sub-registrar's office with the two witnesses and the documents mentioned above.
  • Step 4:Pay the registration fee for the property.
  • Step 5:After 2-3 days in a metropolis and 7 days in a rural area, the sale deed will be ready to collect.

    • Factors Affecting Property Registration Charges

    The factors affecting property registration charges in India are the non-uniformity of registration fees due to the property type, its age, transfer, location, and owner's gender.

    Benefits of Property Registration

    The benefits of property registration are mentioned below.

  • You receive legal validation upon property registration.
  • Title searches become easier with property registration.

  • You are eligible for income tax deductions on taxable property.
  • The process of property mutation (transfer of ownership) becomes easier.
